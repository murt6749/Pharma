Summary of Key Concepts in Steroids

  • Definition: Steroids are naturally occurring organic compounds and their derivatives, playing vital roles in biology, medicine, and chemistry.
  • Structure: They share a common gonane nucleus (17 carbons in four rings: A, B, C, and D).
  • Stereochemistry: Variations such as cis and trans isomers affect the 3D shape and biological activity.
  • Numbering: A fixed carbon numbering system aids in nomenclature and modification.
  • Biosynthesis: Derived from cholesterol through a multi-step pathway (acetate → mevalonate → squalene → lanosterol → cholesterol).
  • Key Enzymes: Enzymes such as HMG-CoA reductase and CYP11A1 are essential in steroid biosynthesis.
  • Cholesterol Sources: Cells synthesize cholesterol de novo, uptake LDL, and store it in lipid droplets.
  • Steroid Hormones: Include glucocorticoids, mineralocorticoids, androgens, estrogens, and progestogens.
  • Transport & Action: Steroid hormones are lipid soluble and circulate bound to specific binding proteins.
  • Clinical Relevance: Synthetic steroids and cholesterol regulation are key in treating various diseases.
